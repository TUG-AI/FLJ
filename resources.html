Video Resources

These videos provide historical and theological context to help you get more out of each week's reading. We recommend watching them before the reading and discussion (~30 minutes or less per week).

Week 1: The Key to Holy Week & Palm Sunday

Objective: Set the stage — understand Roman occupation, why Jews expected a military messiah, and the contrast between Pilate's and Jesus's entries into Jerusalem.
  • Bible Project: 1 Maccabees
    ~8 minutes

    Overview of the Maccabean revolt against Greek/Syrian rule, which shaped Jewish expectations of liberation and the Messiah.

    Watch Video →
  • Bible Project: 2 Maccabees
    ~7 minutes

    Explores themes of martyrdom, God's deliverance, and faithfulness under persecution.

    Watch Video →
  • Bible Project: The Ethic of an Exile
    ~10 minutes

    Discusses Jesus in the context of Roman occupation and his response to those expecting a military messiah.

    Watch Video →
  • Unpacked for Educators: Judea Under Roman Rule
    ~10 minutes

    Covers the period from 63 BCE through the Temple's destruction in 70 CE and the legacy of Jewish resistance.

    Watch Video →

Week 2: Monday – The Whip of Christ

Objective: Understand the Temple's role — economic, political, religious — so the "cleansing" makes sense; grasp the Court of the Gentiles and prophetic sign-acts.
  • Bible Project: Temple
    ~6 minutes

    Explains the Temple as where "heaven and earth" overlap; covers the design and cosmic symbolism of God's dwelling place.

    Watch Video →
  • Bible Project: Heaven and Earth
    ~5 minutes

    The biblical vision that heaven and earth were meant to overlap, helping explain why desecrating the Temple was so serious.

    Watch Video →
  • Messages of Christ: Jerusalem's Temple
    ~15 minutes

    Stunning 3D reconstruction of Herod's Temple based on latest archaeology; visualize the courts and scale Jesus walked through.

    Visit Channel →
